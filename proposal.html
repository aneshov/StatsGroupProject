Description

The dataset contains monthly counts of guests and overnight stays in Munich, split by domestic and international visitors.

Each row represents one tourism observation for a specific combination of tourism measure (MONATSZAHL), visitor origin (AUSPRAEGUNG), year (JAHR), and month or yearly total (MONAT).

Key variables

Variable Meaning Type
MONATSZAHL Metric type: guests or overnight stays chr
AUSPRAEGUNG Visitor origin: domestic / international / total chr
JAHR Year of observation dbl
MONAT Month of observation, or "Summe" for yearly totals chr
WERT Tourism value, either monthly value or yearly total depending on MONAT dbl

Dimensions

Dimension Value
Rows 1,632
Columns 10

Identification variable

The dataset contains an _id column, which can be used as a row identifier.

For the substantive tourism observations, each row can also be identified by the combination of:

MONATSZAHL + AUSPRAEGUNG + JAHR + MONAT

This combination describes the tourism measure, origin category, year, and month or yearly total for each observation.

Fit with project requirements

The dataset meets the project requirements because it has more than 50 rows, a numeric outcome variable (WERT), a numeric explanatory variable (JAHR), and categorical explanatory variables (MONATSZAHL and AUSPRAEGUNG).

Research questions

Research question 1

How do monthly tourism patterns in Munich vary across the year, and which months show the highest and lowest tourism activity?

Claim type: Descriptive / exploratory.
We describe seasonal patterns and identify months with high or low tourism activity.

Research question 2

How did Munich tourism change during the COVID-19 period, and how did domestic and international tourism recover afterwards?

Claim type: Descriptive / exploratory.
We describe visible changes during and after COVID-19, without making strong causal claims.

Research question 3

How do domestic and international tourism differ in their long-term trends and seasonal patterns?

Claim type: Exploratory.
We compare Inland and Ausland to see whether their trends and seasonal patterns differ.

Context and target audience

Our project is framed in a journalistic / public-interest context. We want to explore how tourism in Munich has changed over time, especially seasonal patterns, the COVID-19 period, and differences between domestic and international tourism.

The target audience is a general audience interested in Munich, such as local residents, students, journalists, city administration, and tourism-related businesses. The analysis should therefore be understandable without advanced statistical knowledge.

Suitable presentation formats include:

  • a short data report
  • a Quarto document with explanatory text and visualisations
  • line charts showing trends over time
  • seasonal plots comparing months
  • summary tables comparing domestic and international tourism

Notes on data quality issues

Several data quality issues are already visible in the initial exploration.

First, the variable MONAT contains both monthly values such as "202401" and yearly totals marked as "Summe". These should not be mixed without care, because yearly totals are much larger than monthly observations.

Second, WERT has 72 missing values. These missing values appear especially relevant for 2026, which is likely incomplete at the time of data publication.

Third, the variables VERAEND_VORMONAT_PROZENT and ZWOELF_MONATE_MITTELWERT have more missing values than WERT. This is expected because percentage changes and moving averages cannot always be calculated for early or incomplete observations.

Fourth, AUSPRAEGUNG includes "insgesamt", which is the total of "Inland" and "Ausland". Therefore, "insgesamt" should not be treated as an independent third group when comparing domestic and international tourism.

Finally, MONAT is stored as a character variable. For seasonal analysis, we will likely need to extract the month number from values such as "202401".

Analysis plan

Research question 1

Question:
How do monthly tourism patterns in Munich vary across the year, and which months show the highest and lowest tourism activity?

Variables used

Variable Meaning
WERT Tourism value
MONAT Month / time variable
MONATSZAHL Tourism measure: Gäste or Übernachtungen
AUSPRAEGUNG Origin category: Inland, Ausland, insgesamt

Analytical approach

We will use descriptive statistics and visualisations. We plan to calculate average tourism values for each month and compare seasonal patterns between Gäste and Übernachtungen. Line plots or bar charts by month are appropriate because the question focuses on seasonal patterns across the year.

Cleaning / transformation steps

We will need to extract the month number from MONAT, because values such as 202401 include both year and month. Rows where MONAT = "Summe" will need to be handled separately because they are yearly totals, not monthly observations. For this research question, we will likely exclude "Summe" rows.

Uncertainties / risks

The main risk is mixing monthly values with yearly totals. Another issue is that missing values, especially in incomplete years such as 2026, could distort average monthly patterns. We may need to exclude incomplete years or missing WERT values.

Research question 2

Question:
How did Munich tourism change during the COVID-19 period, and how did domestic and international tourism recover afterwards?

Variables used

Variable Meaning
WERT Tourism value
JAHR Year
MONATSZAHL Gäste or Übernachtungen
AUSPRAEGUNG Inland, Ausland, insgesamt

Analytical approach

We will use time trend analysis with line plots and summary tables. We plan to compare tourism values before, during, and after the COVID-19 period. This can be done by comparing years such as pre-2020, 2020-2021, and post-2021. The approach is descriptive and exploratory, not fully causal.

Cleaning / transformation steps

We will need to decide whether to use monthly observations or yearly totals. For an annual trend analysis, we can either use MONAT = "Summe" or calculate yearly totals from monthly rows. We should not mix both. We will also need to handle missing values and possibly exclude 2026 because it is incomplete.

Uncertainties / risks

The main limitation is that the dataset alone cannot prove that COVID-19 caused the observed changes. It can only show changes during the COVID-19 period. Other factors, such as travel restrictions, economic conditions, or event cancellations, are not included as variables. International and domestic tourism may also be affected by different external factors.

Research question 3

Question:
How do domestic and international tourism differ in their long-term trends and seasonal patterns?

Variables used

Variable Meaning
WERT Tourism value
JAHR Year
MONAT Month
AUSPRAEGUNG Inland and Ausland
MONATSZAHL Gäste and Übernachtungen

Analytical approach

We will compare Inland and Ausland using grouped line plots, seasonal plots, and summary statistics. This allows us to see whether domestic and international tourism follow similar or different patterns over time and across months.

Cleaning / transformation steps

For this question, we will focus on AUSPRAEGUNG = "Inland" and AUSPRAEGUNG = "Ausland". The category "insgesamt" should not be treated as an independent third group because it is the total of domestic and international tourism. We will also extract the month number from MONAT for seasonal analysis and handle missing WERT values.

Uncertainties / risks

The main risk is interpreting differences too strongly. The dataset shows patterns, but it does not include reasons for those patterns. For example, changes in international tourism could be related to travel restrictions, global economic conditions, flight availability, or major events in Munich, but these variables are not included in the dataset.

Main conventions:

  • use snake_case for object and variable names
  • use clear and meaningful names

.gitignore

Our .gitignore file was generated from the standard templates for R, Python, Windows, macOS, and Linux, and extended with Quarto-specific entries.

We exclude operating-system files such as .DS_Store, Thumbs.db, and Linux temporary files. We also exclude R session files such as .Rhistory, .RData, and .Rproj.user/, because they are user-specific and should not be shared through Git.

For reproducibility and security, we exclude local environment files such as .env, .Renviron, virtual environments, cache folders, and temporary build files. Since the project uses Quarto, we also exclude generated Quarto folders and cache files such as /.quarto/, /_site/, /*_files/, and /*_cache/.

Rendered output files and cached intermediate files should be regenerated from the Quarto source document instead of being manually tracked in Git.

#### Variables used

| Variable      | Meaning                          |
|---------------|----------------------------------|
| `WERT`        | Tourism value                    |
| `JAHR`        | Year                             |
| `MONATSZAHL`  | `Gäste` or `Übernachtungen`      |
| `AUSPRAEGUNG` | `Inland`, `Ausland`, `insgesamt` |

#### Analytical approach

We will use time trend analysis with line plots and summary tables. We plan to compare tourism values before, during, and after the COVID-19 period. This can be done by comparing years such as pre-2020, 2020-2021, and post-2021. The approach is descriptive and exploratory, not fully causal.

#### Cleaning / transformation steps

We will need to decide whether to use monthly observations or yearly totals. For an annual trend analysis, we can either use `MONAT = "Summe"` or calculate yearly totals from monthly rows. We should not mix both. We will also need to handle missing values and possibly exclude 2026 because it is incomplete.

#### Uncertainties / risks

The main limitation is that the dataset alone cannot prove that COVID-19 caused the observed changes. It can only show changes during the COVID-19 period. Other factors, such as travel restrictions, economic conditions, or event cancellations, are not included as variables. International and domestic tourism may also be affected by different external factors.

### Research question 3

**Question:**\
How do domestic and international tourism differ in their long-term trends and seasonal patterns?

#### Variables used

| Variable      | Meaning                      |
|---------------|------------------------------|
| `WERT`        | Tourism value                |
| `JAHR`        | Year                         |
| `MONAT`       | Month                        |
| `AUSPRAEGUNG` | `Inland` and `Ausland`       |
| `MONATSZAHL`  | `Gäste` and `Übernachtungen` |

#### Analytical approach

We will compare `Inland` and `Ausland` using grouped line plots, seasonal plots, and summary statistics. This allows us to see whether domestic and international tourism follow similar or different patterns over time and across months.

#### Cleaning / transformation steps

For this question, we will focus on `AUSPRAEGUNG = "Inland"` and `AUSPRAEGUNG = "Ausland"`. The category `"insgesamt"` should not be treated as an independent third group because it is the total of domestic and international tourism. We will also extract the month number from `MONAT` for seasonal analysis and handle missing `WERT` values.

#### Uncertainties / risks

The main risk is interpreting differences too strongly. The dataset shows patterns, but it does not include reasons for those patterns. For example, changes in international tourism could be related to travel restrictions, global economic conditions, flight availability, or major events in Munich, but these variables are not included in the dataset.
